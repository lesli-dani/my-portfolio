About

Everything slightly interesting about me.

Early Life

I was born and raised in Mexico, right on the border to the United States. My sister is almost 3 years older than me, she is also an engineer. In fact, I come from a family full of engineers. Both of my parents are engineers and some of my aunts, uncles, and cousins are engineers too.

Education

I started school in pre-k. It was the only year I ever did in Mexico, the rest of my school life has been in the US. I was in the same school for ten years before going to a different high school (only because my old school didn't have a high school probably). After a fun four years of high school, where I found out I liked electrical and computer engineering and met my two besties, I moved on to college. I'm currently an undergrad student at UTRGV in South Texas pursuing a Computer Engineering degree.

Hobbies

One of my defining traits is that I have a billion hobbies. I love being involved and always having something to do. Although sometimes it's a challenge to have so many hobbies, it has taught me to be extremely organized and I am now a master at time management (although there's always room for improvement!). Projects

Some of my projects, computer engineering related and not.

  • January 2017

    Started a book club in high school

    Growing up I devoured books as much as possible, but in high school I realized that I didn't read as often as I used to. I knew that some of my friends liked to read so I gathered some more people and created the first ever book club at my school. We read a book monthly, discussed them, and sometimes I would plan activites based off of the books!

  • Fall 2018

    My first electrical project

    Before this point I didn't really know what I wanted to study in college, but after realizing how much I enjoyed this project it changed my life. My friend and I created a functioning solar-powered portable USB charger. We had so much fun making it and when it finally worked we were elated!

  • Summer 2019

    First Python course

    This was the summer I decided on Computer Engineering as my major. I knew close to nothing about it and I figured I should probably do something about it so I wouldn't be as clueless my first semester. I took my first ever programming course. I chose Python because I had heard that it was beginner-friendly.

  • Summer 2021

    Summer Internship
